What is Social Engineering?

Social engineering is the tactical weaponization of human psychology. Instead of probing software infrastructure exploits, adversaries leverage trust patterns, authority, panic, or confusion to make users execute critical mistakes willingly.

Common Scam Techniques

Tactics include Pretexting (fabricating false scenarios), Phishing (deceptive communication nodes), Baiting (promising free rewards), and Quid Pro Quo (offering fake services for target tokens or network privileges).

Real-Life Prevention Tips

Slow down whenever communications pressure you to act fast. Always cross-examine validation parameters through standard separate networks. If an administrative office requests access codes, instantly step away and process a dedicated ticket.
